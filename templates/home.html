Our History

History

The rich and illustrious history of wooden frames for sunglasses and spectacles can be traced back to ancient civilizations, where the noble and regal individuals adorned themselves with the finest materials. In the opulent courts of Egypt, Greece, and Rome, wooden frames were carved with intricate designs, embellished with precious stones, and worn as a symbol of status and prestige. As time progressed, the art of crafting wooden frames for eyewear reached new heights of sophistication and elegance. In the Renaissance era, skilled artisans created ornate frames that were both functional and aesthetically pleasing, inspiring generations of designers to come. Today, wooden frames continue to embody the timeless charm and refinement of their storied past, serving as a testament to the enduring beauty and elegance of this age-old craft.
